Introduction¶

For this Project, I chose the 'no-show' Dataset from Kaggle, describing medical Patients charasteristics and whether or not they showed up to the Appointment(s) they had arranged. I want to investigate which of the Characteristics of the Patients influence the Result of showing up to their Appointment(s). The goal could be to be able to predict wether a Patient would be inclined to miss its Appointment and find a Strategy to prevent this.¶

I will investigate specifically the independent Variables for the Age, Gender and Location and try and find if they have an Influence on the Dependent Variable that we are analyzing.¶

Limitations and Conclusion¶

Limitations:¶

If I could find and show some Tendencies for Patients being more inclined to miss their Appointments, the Factors I was investigating, given the Dataset provided, did not reveal a strong Correlation to the Outcome. Furthermore, I think additional and more specific Factors, describing the Patients, were missing in this Dataset, that could help explaining why an important Proportion of medical Appointments were not attended.

What I imagine, could prevent a Patient to show up at a specific Hospital, could be Factors related to the Distance/Journey this Patient has to travel, for example. So, Information like "how far the patient live from the Hospital", or the Quality of the Traffic/public Transportation could be precious additional Variables. Also, Informations about the financial Wealth of the Patients and how secure, the Areas they have to go to, could be valuable Information to work with (Regarding financial Wealth Information, we only had a Variable indicating if a Patient is registered in a social Program helping the Families most needing financial Help. And this did not reveal much either in Correlation to the Outcome Variable). Also, I could identify a Tendency for the Patients between 10 and 30, being more inclined, to not show up to their Appointments: it could be interesting to have additional Information about the Patients Situation: Do the Kids/Teens go to School/University? Are the young Adults employed?

Limitation Conclusion:¶

So in Addition to the Fact, that we are already limited, while just working with descriptive Statistics and Visuals (compared to inferential Statistics and machine Learning)and just are able to build up Intuition about possible Explanations and Tendencies, I think the Dataset we had to work with, was missing additional valuable Variables, in Order to show stronger Correlations and more interesting Findings.

Conclusion:¶

I could make the Observation that:

  • Appointments made for Patients of a particular Age level between 10 and 30 years old, tend to be more missed than in all other age levels, so the Age seems to have a Correlation with the Outcome
  • the Gender Characteristic of the Patients does not seem to influence whether their missed their Appointments or not
  • Appointments that were made in certain Neighbourhoods, "ITARAR", "JESUS DE NAZARETH" and "CARATORA" tend to be more "missed" than in the 2 main Neighbourhoods for Example
